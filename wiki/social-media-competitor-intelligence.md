# Social Media Competitor Intelligence

## Instagram Public-Data Reports

The Instagram competitor intelligence repository provides a developer-oriented starter kit for weekly competitor reports. It tracks public brand accounts, ranks Reels and posts, extracts hashtags, finds creator mentions, exports CSVs, and generates Markdown/HTML reports. It explicitly avoids follower automation, private account monitoring, login automation, bulk messaging, or spam.

The design is useful because it separates mock data, provider adapters, report templates, cost controls, scheduling, and integrations. This makes the workflow adaptable for agencies, DTC brands, and analysts.

## LinkedIn Listening

The Gumloop workflow source describes LinkedIn signal monitoring: define keywords, fetch LinkedIn data, parse author/content/engagement fields, generate on-brand comments and post ideas, then review everything in Airtable. The core output is not just monitoring; it is a daily opportunity queue for sales, thought leadership, and competitor awareness.

## YouTube Idea Research

The Claude Skills Notion source describes a YouTube idea finder that uses search volume, competitor videos, and gap analysis to choose an angle, title, and thumbnail concept. This is the pre-production version of competitor intelligence: understand demand and existing supply before creating.

## Broader Creator and Content Monitoring

The OpenClaw use-case list includes daily Reddit digests, YouTube digests, X account analysis, multi-source tech news, X/Twitter automation, YouTube content pipelines, multi-agent content factories, podcast production, and creator/social workflows. Printing Press also includes `pp-scrape-creators`, a CLI concept for scraping public social data across major platforms.

Together, these sources suggest a product opportunity: an agent-assisted content intelligence system that monitors public channels, ranks signals, drafts responses, and produces periodic reports.
