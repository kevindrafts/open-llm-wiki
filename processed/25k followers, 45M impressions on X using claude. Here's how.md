### 2\. Timing beats quality. Every time.

Corey's article on Karpathy's "second brain" concept got **1.7 million views in two days.** Not because it was the best article ever written. Because at the moment it went live, there were only 6 or 7 total articles on that topic on X and the trend was just starting to accelerate.

He uses a free skill called **[Last 30 Days](https://github.com/mvanhorn/last30days-skill?utm_source=thevibemarketer.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=25k-followers-45m-impressions-on-x-using-claude-here-s-how)** (GitHub, by Matt Van Horn). It scrapes X, Reddit, and YouTube to surface what's beginning to trend before it blows up. He runs it every week, picks one topic that feels interesting, blocks a few hours to go deep on it, and writes.

Being two weeks early beats being twice as good.

But, what if the topics you want to talk about aren't trending?

You're not building your niche around trends. You're using trends to get your niche in front of more people. The trend is the vehicle. Your expertise is the destination.

==***But, knowing what to write about is only half the battle. The other half is how fast you can ship it.***==

### 3\. One article. A week of content. 20 minutes.

Corey runs his entire content operation on 4 skills stacked together:

**Layer 1 — Brand Voice Skill**  
The foundation. You feed it your best posts, podcast transcripts, YouTube recordings. It learns how you write, the words you use, the ones you avoid, your tone. Nothing else in the stack works without this.

**Layer 2 — X Article Writer Skill**  
Takes a topic or transcript and writes a full 1,000–2,000 word article in your voice, with research. The second brain article that got 1.7M views? Written with this. Shipped in under 20 minutes.

**Layer 3 — Quote Tweet Writer Skill**  
Takes the article, pulls the 3–5 sharpest points, turns each into a standalone quote tweet. One article becomes five more pieces of content — one per day for the rest of the week.

**Layer 4 — Infographic Brief Skill**  
Paired with each quote tweet. Generates a detailed prompt you paste into ChatGPT Image 2, which produces a polished infographic. Rotates between 12 different styles so nothing looks repetitive.

He built an orchestrator skill that runs layers 3 and 4 in parallel one paste in, five quote tweets and five infographic prompts come out the other side.

Now, if the content is fully AI written, will it actually be accurate?

Corey’s method does not replace the editing process.  
  
He says, “Always audit. Check formatting, verify sources, read it like a reader would. AI gets you to 80–90%. The last 10% is on you.”  
  
==***One thing to keep in mind: none of this matters if the views don't convert into something you actually own.***==

### 4\. The algorithm will change. Your list won't.

Corey stated his views have dipped, spiked and dipped again. That's just how it goes.

But his email list kept growing through all of it. Close to 14,000 for his company, 8,500 personal subscribers.

His strategy is almost annoyingly simple:

- Build an inventory of lead magnets across your main topics. Don't create a new one for every post, just mix and match what you already have
- When a post starts gaining momentum **within the first 6–8 hours** reply with a lead magnet directly relevant to that post. Not a generic freebie. One that matches exactly what the post is about
- In every long article, place a relevant lead magnet **above the fold** before the content starts. Most people never scroll to the bottom

One lead magnet. One article. Close to 1,000 email signups.

==***And, the fastest way to build that list? Show up before you feel ready.***==

### 5\. You only need to be one chapter ahead

Someone in the chat wrote: ***"I feel like I need 10 years of experience before I can speak on this."***

Just like DiCaprio in Catch Me If You Can — ***"I just needed to be one chapter ahead of the class."***

That's the whole bar. One week ahead of the person you're talking to. Not an expert. Just further along.

But, a dying niche will grind you down no matter how good your content is. A growing niche pulls you forward even when your content isn't perfect.

==***The next key step? Pick the right room before you worry about how loud to speak.***==

### 6\. Niche down until it feels too specific

AI is too broad. If you're talking about everything AI, you'll get lost in the noise.

Corey's bio says: *simplifying AI for non-technical entrepreneurs.* He even goes further $500K to $5M in revenue is who he pictures when he sits down to write. That level of specificity is what makes content feel like it was written for someone, not for everyone.

The tighter the niche, the lower the bar to be seen as the go-to person. If you're talking to real estate agents about AI they're barely using ChatGPT. You don't need to be an AI expert. You just need to know more than they do.

==***Once you know who you're talking to, the next question is — what do you talk about?***==

### 7\. Ride the wave, but don't chase every one

The game isn't jumping from trend to trend. It's finding one early, going deep on it, and weaving what you learned into the next one.

Corey rode the OpenClaw wave early. That one trend was the springboard for 3 months of growth. He didn't reinvent himself when the next thing came along he took the principles, the audience, the credibility, and carried them forward.

I do the same thing. I started talking about how to use AI with marketing, then moved into workflows, then Claude Code. It's not reinventing yourself. It's repurposing your use cases into whatever is the more efficient or relevant way to do it with the latest tool. You're just documenting and sharing what you're already figuring out.  
  
That is the content strategy. The best content isn't planned. It's documented.

==***Documenting the right thing in the wrong format is still a miss. And this is where you pay attention to algorithm…***==

### 8\. Formats also matters

Right now, plain text posts on X don't perform. The algorithm is rewarding video specifically long-form, even unpolished. A 12-minute Loom recording broke through more than a perfectly written article.

Two months ago it was meme quote tweets. Before that, threads. It changes constantly.

**Corey's approach:** make a list of the top 15-20 creators in your niche. Watch what formats they're using. If 80% of the winning content is video right now — that's your answer. Adapt your perspective and voice to the format that's working. Don't fight the algorithm.

Copy the format. Never copy the content.

==***Because, format is the container and what you put in that makes or breaks your content strategy.***==

### 9\. AI is an accelerant. Taste is still yours.

AI makes you faster. It does not make you better at things you haven't put in the work to understand.

Here’s what I think: *"AI is a great accelerant. But you still need taste. You develop taste by spending time in it like: reading what works, understanding patterns, knowing when something's good before you hit publish.*  
  
*AI makes you better at what you're already good at. It makes you worse at what you're bad at. Knowing when not to use it matters more than knowing when to use it."*

You develop taste by spending time in it every day. Reading what works. Becoming the practitioner you claim to be. Understanding why. Knowing when something's good before you publish it. That part isn't automated.

==***And, as the saying goes: the hammer doesn't make the carpenter.***==
