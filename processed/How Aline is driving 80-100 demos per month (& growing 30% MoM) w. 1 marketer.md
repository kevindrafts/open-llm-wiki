## Cold email at scale

There are ~150,000 in-house legal and double the amount of secondary personas creating a list of 300K+ (think CFOs, procurement, COOs, contract touching folks) in Ian’s TAM. He emails 20,000/mo with a one-email sequence per persona. No crazy personalization, a couple of variants, but he breaks out key personas where the message needs to be meaningfully different.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xrw0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F73ac8233-a7ca-4931-bfb0-3667da4e8f80_2048x509.png)

To do this:

- He built his list in Clay using Apollo, Prospeo, and Hunter, then verifying through Neverbounce and Zerobounce.
- He sends through EmailBison, which handles inbox set up and warming.
- Weekly he’ll ship new experiments.
- And every four months, he refreshes his TAM list, and restarts the process.

This is booking 50%+ of his demos.

## Vibecoded signal-based prospecting agents

Cold email covers the broad TAM. These two agents go the other direction. 10 hand-picked accounts a day, with a 3-touch sequence drafted and ready for review every morning.

To do this:

- The prospecting agent scans daily for fresh funding rounds, legal hires, and tech stack changes from the last 7-14 days.
- Before spending a Clay credit, it checks HubSpot. Already in sequence, recent meeting, or in an active outbound cohort = skip and source a replacement of the same tier.
- Clay finds the contacts. Apollo verifies the emails and LinkedIn URLs. No pattern guessing (first.last@domain is wrong ~90% of the time in his data).
- The personalization agent then grabs that day’s fresh batch and drafts a 3-touch sequence per contact. Touch 1 on an operational trigger. Touch 2 a new angle. Touch 3 a customer result plus a $100 Amazon gift card for 15 minutes.
- Voice is the whole game. Short paragraphs, 3-word lowercase subject lines, no raise-anchored openers, reads like a peer texting a colleague.
- A validator catches em dashes, oversized subjects, raise-anchor openers, and duplicate customer stories before anything touches HubSpot.
- Then it stages the whole batch into HubSpot’s sequence enrollment UI. Stops one click before Enroll. Ian reviews and clicks Enroll himself.

This is driving roughly 10% of pipeline today with only hitting the “Enroll” button in Hubspot.

## SEO/AEO and Meta (the bets in flight)

**SEO/GEO experiment: the non-commodity content grader on top of SEO Agency**

This one’s still an experiment. Ian came across a Perplexity walkthrough from Chris Long at Nectiv ([link](https://www.perplexity.ai/computer/a/commodity-vs-non-commodity-a-v-QBTbTgcaTdSBWAnXBlp.mg)) that turned Danny Sullivan’s commodity vs non-commodity framework into a six-dimension content rubric. He wired the rubric up to Aline’s tools.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RPnS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F927e82df-7cd1-4893-92bc-1f1a63a772d6_2048x920.png)

Aline’s SEO/GEO agency handles the bulk of content writing (brief, draft, publish). The agent runs on top to further tune each piece for SEO/GEO before it ships.

To do this:

- Every draft gets scored against the six dimensions: Proprietary Evidence, Firsthand Experience, Specificity, POV, LLM Moat, Information Gain.
- The agent flags every “studies show” hedge, every “experts recommend” framing, every AI-default H2.
- For weak drafts, it runs a surgical rewrite loop. Swap “according to a survey” for an Aline HubSpot number. Swap “your team may struggle with X” for a verbatim Fireflies quote from a recent customer call. Add a contrarian POV section where there wasn’t one.
- Every draft clears a top-3 SERP gap check. The agent reads the top 3 Google results for the headline and confirms the draft adds at least 3 facts those results don’t already cover.
- Nothing auto-publishes. Ian reviews every scored draft before it ships.
- Here is an example of an output - [https://www.aline.co/post/contract-negotiation-software](https://www.aline.co/post/contract-negotiation-software)

Next up: he’s roadmapping markdown versions of every key landing page so ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini can crawl Aline content directly. As search shifts from Google to LLM answers, the citation inside the model’s response is the new ranking #1.

**Meta ads for lawyers bet (with FlyTech)**

For most of the last year, Ian ran a small Meta retargeting play himself. $50/day max, 18 creative variants, mostly aimed at influence and brand halo, not direct lead gen. It did what it was supposed to do (keep Aline in front of warm audiences) and not much more.

He’s now graduating to agency-led lead gen with FlyTech. They’re a paid-social agency that’s been specialized in legal tech for four years and brought a real lead gen proposal to the table.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6I_R!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcc5835de-9e9d-4dda-a5ea-13f70ee4144a_1742x702.png)

To do this:

- Dropped the self-managed retargeting campaign to $1/day to preserve learnings without burning spend.
- They rebuilt creative from scratch and launched a fresh test batch at $50/day. New angles include “ChatGPT for Law” framing and zero data retention as the differentiator versus Harvey.
- Early returns are looking up. Click-through on the new creative is 3 to 4%, above benchmark. Data’s still light but the signal is there.
- Weekly sync to review what’s working and reload creative.
- Also, [we did a LinkedIn live](https://www.linkedin.com/events/7462524951229804545?viewAsMember=true) where we redlined their contract live using Aline AI. A “How Aline uses Aline” session per se.

Why bet on Meta at all when legal supposedly lives on LinkedIn? Ian keeps hearing from prospects that legal is on Meta too. Not their first scroll, but they’re there. Worth $50/day to find out.
